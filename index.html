Field notes for responsible visitors

Invasive species prevention starts with cleaning all watercraft, trailers, and fishing equipment before moving between water bodies. Remove all visible plant material, drain water from boats and live wells, and allow equipment to dry completely before launching in new locations.

Respecting wildlife means maintaining appropriate distances from nesting areas, avoiding loud noises during sensitive periods like spring spawning, and properly storing food to prevent attracting animals to campsites. Loons, in particular, require quiet zones during nesting season.

Water sprite habitat preservation depends on avoiding motor operation in shallow areas where propellers can damage plant communities. Use depth finders or local knowledge to identify sensitive zones, and consider paddle-powered alternatives when exploring areas with dense aquatic vegetation.

Leave no trace principles apply especially around lake ecosystems, where even small amounts of soap, food waste, or human waste can alter water chemistry and affect plant and animal communities. Pack out all trash and use designated facilities when available.
